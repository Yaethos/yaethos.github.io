Detective Kortenaer's Escape is the first *narrative EP* (name sounds cool but really means nothing) I've produced. I wrote and published it in January 2025, in a rush before moving to Oslo, Norway for my ERASMUS. The matrix of the project was the momentarily possession of an electric bass, a kind gift from the bassist for [SUPER VIOLENT EXPLODING SUN](../SUPER-VIOLENT-EXPLODING-SUN) that was moving for her ERASMUS at the time.

The tracks originated as casual compositions, unrelated style exercises born out of curiosity for a new instrument. Relistening to the tracks however, I eventually managed to find some sort of narrative, woven through the use of common instruments. The image of a town shook by a murder, serving as the backdrop for the internal meandering of a broken investigator is by no means an original locus. The main inspiration here is clearly [Disco Elysium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disco_Elysium), with the name *"Kortenaer"* being the last name of the victim in the game. 

The narrative has voluntarily been left ambiguous, appearing through the interpretation of instrument choices and, most explicitly, through the canvas uploaded on spotify (*wacky choice, would not do it again*). With limited knowledge of both animation and poetry, the canvas have been created as free-verse poems in squigglevision, overlaid on liquid marble animations. For the sake of conservation, I'll upload the canvas here as well.
